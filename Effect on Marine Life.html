Effect on Marine Life

Plastic pollution poses a severe threat to marine life, from entanglement to ingestion. Understanding how different species are impacted can help us advocate for their protection.

Sea Turtles

Sea turtles mistake plastic bags for jellyfish, leading to fatal blockages. Nearly 50% of sea turtles have ingested plastic, which can cause internal injuries.

Seabirds

Plastic fragments are often found in the stomachs of seabirds. Ingested plastic can block digestion, causing malnutrition, and affects over 90% of seabird species.

Whales

Whales can consume hundreds of pounds of plastic, mistaking it for food. This can lead to blockages and starvation, as plastic fills their stomachs without providing nutrition.

Impact on Marine Ecosystems

Plastic pollution also disrupts entire ecosystems, affecting food chains, marine habitats, and biodiversity.

Coral Reefs

Plastic can carry invasive species and diseases to coral reefs, weakening these fragile ecosystems, which are essential for marine biodiversity.

Fish Population

Plastic ingestion affects fish reproduction and growth, impacting the food supply for larger predators and humans who rely on fish as a food source.

Seaweed Beds

Seaweed beds that are essential for nutrient cycling can be smothered by plastic, reducing their ability to support marine life and contribute to a healthy ocean.
