About Plastic Waste

Plastic waste is one of the most pressing environmental challenges of our time. Over the past few decades, the production and consumption of plastic have skyrocketed, with an estimated 300 million tons of plastic produced globally each year. A significant portion of this plastic ends up in our oceans, where it can take hundreds of years to decompose.

Sources of Plastic Waste

Single-Use Plastics: Items like straws, bags, and bottles are used for a short period but can persist in the environment for centuries.

Microplastics: These tiny particles come from the breakdown of larger plastic items and are prevalent in cosmetics and clothing.

Industrial Waste: Plastic debris from manufacturing processes often escapes into waterways.

Consequences of Plastic Waste

Ecosystem Disruption: Plastic can smother coral reefs, alter habitats, and lead to loss of biodiversity.

Economic Costs: Marine debris affects fishing, tourism, and navigation, resulting in substantial economic losses.
